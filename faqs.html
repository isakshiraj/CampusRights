What is CampusRights? ×
CampusRights is a platform dedicated to educating college students about their legal rights on campus. It covers key areas like academic integrity, financial aid, privacy, and harassment, aiming to create a safer and more informed academic environment.
What is the Vision, Mission, and Objective of CampusRights? ×
Vision: To empower students with knowledge about their legal rights, fostering a confident and responsible campus community.
Mission: To educate students on their rights and responsibilities, promoting fairness, transparency, and support within the institution.
Objective: To raise awareness, promote fairness, enhance support, encourage transparency, and empower students to navigate their academic environment effectively.
What is Academic Integrity and why is it important? ×
Academic integrity refers to the commitment to maintaining honesty and fairness in academic work. It involves avoiding cheating, plagiarism, and other dishonest practices. Upholding academic integrity is crucial because it ensures a fair educational environment and helps students develop genuine knowledge and skills. Violations can lead to serious consequences such as failing grades or expulsion.
What are considered "Unfair Means" during examinations? ×
Unfair means include any form of dishonesty during exams, such as:
  • Assisting or receiving help from others.
  • Using unauthorized materials or devices.
  • Carrying or using prohibited items like books or notes.
  • Impersonating another candidate.
  • Communicating with others or attempting to influence examiners.
  • Destroying or concealing evidence of cheating.
What are the penalties for using unfair means? ×
Penalties for using unfair means can include expulsion from the examination center, legal action under the Indian Penal Code, and debarment from future exams. Serious cases may lead to expulsion from the university.
What are the attendance requirements for students? ×
Students must maintain a minimum of 75% attendance in all courses. The Dean can condone up to 10% attendance shortage for valid reasons, and the Vice Chancellor may allow an additional 5% under exceptional circumstances, such as illness or authorized activities.
What criteria must be met to pass a course? ×
To pass a course, students need to obtain at least 50% marks in aggregate, including both the end-term exam and continuous evaluation by the teacher. Students can apply for rechecking if they believe there are discrepancies in their results.
What financial aid options are available for IGDTUW students? ×
IGDTUW offers various scholarships, including:
  • IGDTUW JRF/SRF (TRF) Scholarship for PhD students.
  • Financial assistance for M.Tech. and M.Plan. students.
  • Scholarships like the Upma Memorial Award, Dr. Babuajee Jha Scholarship, and others for different student categories.
What are privacy rights and why are they important for college students? ×
Privacy rights involve protections over personal information and how it's used by the college. Students have the right to know what data is collected, how it's stored, and who has access to it. This ensures that students can safeguard their personal and academic data, maintain confidentiality, and make informed decisions about sharing their information.
How can students protect their digital privacy? ×
Students should:
  • Review and understand their college’s privacy policies.
  • Use strong, unique passwords and enable two-factor authentication.
  • Be cautious about sharing personal information online.
  • Regularly check and adjust privacy settings on educational platforms.
What constitutes sexual harassment, and how should it be reported? ×
Sexual harassment includes unwelcome sexual behaviors such as physical contact, requests for sexual favors, sexually colored remarks, and showing pornography. To report sexual harassment:
  • Write a detailed complaint with dates, names, and any supporting documents.
  • Submit the complaint to the ICC office or email iccoffice@igdtuw.ac.in.
  • Contact the Women Helpline Number (181) or Emergency Response Support System Number (112).
What actions should I take if I believe my privacy rights have been violated? ×
If you believe your privacy rights have been violated, you should:
  • Report the issue to the appropriate campus office.
  • Review and correct any inaccuracies in your educational records.
  • Seek guidance from campus resources or legal services.
  • Stay informed about privacy laws and advocate for transparent policies.
